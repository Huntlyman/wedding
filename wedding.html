About Hunt

A Life Built Around
Words & People

As a licensed civil celebrant in Loudoun County, Virginia, I am authorized by the Commonwealth to solemnize marriages. But a ceremony is so much more than a legal formality — it is the most public and personal moment in a couple's life together, and I take that privilege seriously.

With more than four decades of experience as an educator, I have spent my career studying language, literature, and the way words shape our understanding of the world. I know that the right words at the right moment can be transformative. I bring that same care and attentiveness to every ceremony I officiate.

I am a lifelong resident of Middleburg, Virginia — the heart of horse country in the Blue Ridge foothills — and I understand the beauty and character of this region. Whether your ceremony is in a historic Loudoun barn, a vineyard, a garden, or your own backyard, I am at home here.

My approach is collaborative. Before your ceremony, we will talk — about how you met, what you love about each other, what words and readings speak to you, and what kind of ceremony feels most like you. Then I will help craft something memorable.

How It Works

The Shape of a Ceremony

Every ceremony I officiate is built from scratch, in close conversation with you. There is no template, no boilerplate, no ceremony that sounds like it was ordered from a catalogue.

We begin by talking — about your story, your values, and the tone you want to strike. From there, I draft a ceremony script and share it with you for review and revision. Everything is negotiable: the length, the formality, the humor, the tears.

Step One

The Conversation

We meet (in person or by video) to talk through your vision, your story, and any special elements you'd like to include — vows, readings, rituals, music cues.

Step Two

The Draft

I write a custom ceremony script and send it to you for feedback. We revise together until every word feels right.

Step Three

The Rehearsal

I attend your rehearsal to walk through timing, blocking, and any last-minute questions — so your wedding day feels effortless.

Step Four

The Ceremony

I arrive early, prepared, and ready. I guide your ceremony with warmth and attention, and handle all legal paperwork with the Loudoun County courts.

Practical Matters

Logistics & the Marriage License

The ceremony we create together can be as brief or as elaborate as you like. But one thing is non-negotiable: a valid Virginia marriage license, which I must have in hand before we begin. Fortunately, the process in Loudoun County is simple and the office is welcoming.

Your license is valid for 60 days from the date of issue, and there is no waiting period — you can receive it and get married the same day if you choose. After the ceremony, I am responsible for completing both copies and returning them to the Clerk of the Circuit Court within five days.

Beyond the license, there are no blood tests, no residency requirements, and no proof of divorce required. You simply need a government-issued photo ID and the fee (currently $35). Both parties must appear in person or via virtual appointment.

Please don't hesitate to contact me with any questions about the process — I am happy to walk you through it.
